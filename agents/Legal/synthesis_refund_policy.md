## 2. The Nature of the Service and Why Refunds Are Limited

Understanding why Synthesis has a strict no-refund policy requires understanding how the Service works.

When you place an order — whether for a One-Shot Report, an Add-on, or a Subscription — and payment is confirmed, the Synthesis multi-agent AI pipeline is triggered immediately or queued for execution. This pipeline consumes real-time computational resources: nine specialized AI agents sequentially and in parallel process financial filings, market data, earnings call transcripts, news sources, and quantitative models. Each Report generation incurs significant and immediate computational costs borne by Synthesis, regardless of whether the User ultimately reads the delivered Report.

This is fundamentally different from purchasing a physical product or a pre-produced digital file. **The Report is created specifically for you, on demand, at your request, the moment your payment is confirmed.** Once initiated, that cost cannot be recovered.

For this reason, Synthesis's Refund Policy is built on a clear principle: **the User pays for the generation of a Report, not for any particular analytical conclusion.** The Service is delivered the moment generation is initiated.

## 4. The Only Exception: Material Error Remedy

Synthesis acknowledges that, on rare occasions, its AI pipeline may produce a Report that contains an objective, verifiable error of such significance that the delivered product is fundamentally not what the User paid for. In these cases — and only these cases — Synthesis provides a remedy.

### 4.1 Definition of Material Error

A **Material Error** is defined exclusively as:

**(a) Wrong subject — Company substitution error:**
The Report has been generated for a company or security other than the Ticker symbol specified by the User at the time of purchase. For example, the User requested an analysis of ticker `AAPL` and received an analysis of a different company due to a system error.

**(b) Manifest internal incoherence — Irreconcilable factual error:**
The Report contains quantitative or factual statements that are objectively and irreconcilably inconsistent with publicly verifiable data about the specified company, to a degree that renders the Report analytically unusable. This requires the error to be:
- **Objective:** verifiable against a publicly available primary source (SEC filing, exchange data, official company disclosure); and
- **Material:** of sufficient magnitude to fundamentally undermine the analytical integrity of the Report; and
- **Irreconcilable:** not explainable by differences in data source timing, analytical methodology, or reasonable interpretive judgment.

**Illustrative examples of what does and does not constitute a Material Error:**

| Scenario | Material Error? | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Report generated for the wrong company entirely | ✅ Yes | Company substitution error — wrong subject |
| Stated market capitalization is off by 500% with no explanation | ✅ Yes | Irreconcilable factual error against primary source |
| Valuation scenario suggests $350 fair value for a $10 stock with no documented extraordinary basis | ✅ Yes | Manifest incoherence — irreconcilable with basic market data |
| Investment Stance is "Cautious" but the User believes it should be "Constructive" | ❌ No | Analytical disagreement — not a factual error |
| Report uses a P/E multiple the User believes is too high | ❌ No | Methodological disagreement — not an error |
| Valuation range does not match a sell-side consensus the User prefers | ❌ No | Difference of opinion — not an error |
| Report was delivered 30 minutes later than estimated | ❌ No | Delivery delay — not a quality defect |
| A minor financial ratio is slightly different from a third-party source | ❌ No | Minor discrepancy — not material |
| The Report discloses a Quality Score caveat limiting the Stance | ❌ No | Disclosed limitation — not an error |
| Market conditions changed after Report delivery | ❌ No | Post-delivery event — not a Report defect |
| The User purchased a Core Report and wanted Edge-level depth | ❌ No | Tier selection — not a defect |

### 4.2 The Remedy: Regeneration at No Cost

If Synthesis determines that a Material Error has occurred, the remedy is the **regeneration of the Report at no additional cost to the User**, delivering the analysis that the User originally purchased.

**No monetary refund will be issued under any circumstance, including confirmed Material Errors.** The regenerated Report constitutes the User's sole and complete remedy in respect of any deficiency in a delivered Report.

This approach reflects a considered policy decision: the purpose of the Refund Policy is to ensure the User receives the service they paid for. Regeneration achieves this purpose. A monetary refund, by contrast, would leave the User without the analysis and Synthesis bearing the full cost of generation with no commercial recovery.

The regenerated Report:
- Will be generated at the same tier (Core, Prime, or Edge) as the original purchase
- Will be delivered to the same email address as the original order
- Will be generated using the most current available data at the time of regeneration, which may differ from data available at the time of the original generation
- Will be subject to the same Terms and Conditions and this Refund Policy

### 4.3 How to Submit a Material Error Claim

To request a remedy under this Section, the User must:

**Step 1 — Contact Synthesis within 7 days**
Send an email to **[support@synthesis.ai]** within **seven (7) calendar days** of the Report delivery date. Claims submitted after this window will not be accepted, as data conditions change rapidly and it is not possible to assess the accuracy of the original Report against stale market data after an extended period.

**Step 2 — Provide the following information**
Your claim must include:
- The order reference number (included in your confirmation email)
- The email address used for the purchase
- A clear description of the alleged error
- A reference to the specific section(s) of the Report containing the alleged error
- A link to or citation of the publicly available primary source that contradicts the Report's content (e.g., the specific SEC filing, exchange page, or official disclosure)

**Step 3 — Synthesis reviews your claim**
Synthesis will acknowledge your claim within **3 business days** and will complete its review within **10 business days** of acknowledgment. For complex cases, this review period may be extended, in which case Synthesis will notify you.

**Step 4 — Outcome**
- If the claim is **upheld**, Synthesis will initiate regeneration of the Report and notify you when the new Report has been delivered. No further action is required from you.
- If the claim is **not upheld**, Synthesis will provide a written explanation of why the reported issue does not meet the definition of Material Error under Section 4.1.

### 4.4 Limits on Material Error Claims

- A maximum of **one (1) Material Error claim** may be submitted per order.
- If the regenerated Report also contains a claimed error, the User may submit a second claim only if a new and distinct Material Error (as defined in Section 4.1) is present. The same alleged error from the original claim will not be re-reviewed.
- Material Error claims are not available for: Add-on content (News Pulse alerts), Ticker Swap fees, or Subscription fees. They apply exclusively to the analytical Report content (PDF and/or PPTX).

## 5. Subscriptions — Billing, Cancellation, and No Pro-Rata Refunds

### 5.1 Subscription billing

Subscriptions are billed at the beginning of each billing period (monthly or annual). Payment is processed automatically via Stripe on the renewal date using the payment method on file.

### 5.2 Cancellation

The User may cancel a Subscription at any time through:
- The Synthesis dashboard (Settings → Subscription → Cancel); or
- By contacting Synthesis at [support@synthesis.ai].

**Cancellation takes effect at the end of the current paid billing period.** The User retains full access to all Subscription features until that date. No access is revoked upon cancellation — the cancellation simply prevents the next renewal charge.

### 5.3 No pro-rata refunds for mid-period cancellations

**Synthesis does not issue pro-rata refunds for the unused portion of a Subscription period.** If a User cancels a monthly Subscription on day 10 of a 30-day billing cycle, the Subscription remains active for the remaining 20 days and no refund is issued for those days. The same applies to annual Subscriptions.

This policy reflects the fact that Synthesis incurs infrastructure, data, and operational costs for the entire period during which the User has access to the Service, regardless of whether the User actively uses it during that period.

### 5.4 Annual Subscriptions

Annual Subscriptions are charged as a single payment at the beginning of the annual period. If the User cancels an annual Subscription:
- The Subscription remains active until the end of the annual period
- No refund is issued for the remaining months of the annual period
- The User will not be charged for the following annual period

### 5.5 Failed payments and involuntary cancellation

If a Subscription renewal payment fails (e.g., due to an expired card), Stripe will attempt to re-process the payment according to its standard retry schedule. Synthesis will notify the User by email of the payment failure. If payment cannot be collected after all retry attempts, the Subscription will be suspended and access to Subscription features will be restricted until payment is updated or the Subscription is cancelled. No refund applies in this scenario.

### 5.6 Subscription tier changes

Users who upgrade to a higher Subscription tier during an active billing period will be charged the pro-rated difference for the remainder of the current period (handled automatically by Stripe). Downgrading to a lower tier takes effect at the start of the next billing period; no pro-rata credit is issued for the current period.

## 6. EU/EEA Consumer Rights — Right of Withdrawal

### 6.1 General position on the right of withdrawal

Under Directive 2011/83/EU on consumer rights, consumers in the European Union and European Economic Area have a right of withdrawal (right to cancel) within 14 days of conclusion of a contract for digital content, without giving any reason.

**However**, Article 16(m) of Directive 2011/83/EU provides that the right of withdrawal does not apply to contracts for the supply of digital content that is not supplied on a tangible medium, **where performance has begun with the consumer's prior express consent and their acknowledgment that they thereby lose their right of withdrawal.**

**The Report generation process begins immediately upon payment confirmation.** By completing the purchase — including the affirmative acceptance of the pre-purchase checkbox that explicitly draws attention to this condition — EU/EEA Users provide their prior express consent to the immediate commencement of performance and acknowledge that they thereby waive their right of withdrawal under Directive 2011/83/EU.

### 6.2 What this means in practice

For EU/EEA Users who purchase a One-Shot Report:

- The right of withdrawal **does not apply** once Report generation has commenced.
- Report generation commences immediately upon payment confirmation — typically within seconds or minutes.
- The User's acceptance of the pre-purchase checkbox constitutes the legally required express consent to immediate commencement and the consequent waiver of the right of withdrawal.

For EU/EEA Users who purchase a Subscription:

- The right of withdrawal **may apply** for a period of 14 days from the date of conclusion of the Subscription contract, provided that the Subscription's performance has not begun (i.e., no Report has been generated for any Active Ticker during those 14 days).
- If the User requests the immediate activation of the Subscription (i.e., authorises Synthesis to begin generating Reports for Active Tickers immediately upon purchase), the User provides express consent to immediate commencement and thereby waives the right of withdrawal.
- In practice, the Subscription dashboard is available and active immediately upon payment. Users who add Active Tickers immediately after subscription activation have consented to immediate performance and have waived the right of withdrawal.

### 6.3 Statutory rights preserved

Nothing in this Refund Policy shall limit or exclude any statutory rights of EU/EEA consumers that cannot be contractually waived under mandatory applicable law. Specifically, the right to a conformity remedy (repair, replacement, or, where neither is possible, price reduction or rescission) under Directive 2019/770/EU on contracts for the supply of digital content remains available to EU/EEA consumers where the digital content is not in conformity with the contract. Synthesis's Material Error Regeneration remedy (Section 4) is designed to be consistent with this right.

## 7. Accidental Duplicate Purchases

If a User accidentally purchases the same Report (same ticker, same tier) more than once within a short period due to a technical error or accidental duplicate submission, the User should contact Synthesis at **[support@synthesis.ai]** within **48 hours** of the duplicate purchase, providing both order reference numbers.

Synthesis will review the circumstances. If it is evident that a genuine duplicate purchase occurred (same ticker, same tier, near-simultaneous timestamps, and a single Report generation was triggered), Synthesis will, at its discretion, issue a credit of the duplicate payment amount toward a future purchase, or apply it to an existing Subscription. **Monetary refunds for duplicate purchases are not guaranteed** but will be considered on a case-by-case basis.

Duplicate purchases arising from the User deliberately placing multiple orders for the same ticker and tier (e.g., to compare outputs) are not eligible for any remedy.

These records are provided to Stripe and the relevant card network in response to any chargeback dispute.

If a chargeback is successfully disputed by the User in a jurisdiction where this Refund Policy is not fully enforceable, Synthesis's obligation is limited to ensuring the User receives the Report they paid for. Synthesis does not accept chargebacks as a substitute for the Material Error remedy process described in Section 4.
